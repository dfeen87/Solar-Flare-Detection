## Abstract

Solar flares are explosive releases of magnetic energy in the solar corona that can strongly influence the near-Earth space environment. Reliable identification of early flare precursors remains a central challenge for space weather forecasting.

In this work we investigate whether statistical signatures in multi-channel GOES observations provide detectable early indicators of solar flare activity. We analyze soft X-ray flux measurements together with flare event catalogues and complementary observational channels when available.

Pre-event variability is quantified using windowed variance measures and related fluctuation statistics. Based on these quantities we introduce a **composite instability indicator** that combines X-ray variability with surrogate measures of magnetic perturbations.

Within this framework, the pre-flare phase can be interpreted as a non-equilibrium build-up process in which temporal correlations and phase-like degrees of freedom increase prior to the eruptive release of magnetic energy.

> **Keywords:** solar flares · space weather · GOES X-ray observations · magnetic reconnection · flare precursors · instability indicators

## 1 · Introduction

Solar flares are among the most energetic phenomena in the solar atmosphere. They occur when magnetic energy stored in the solar corona is rapidly released through **magnetic reconnection**, producing bursts of electromagnetic radiation, energetic particle acceleration, and large-scale plasma restructuring.

Major flare events and associated coronal mass ejections (CMEs) can strongly influence near-Earth space-weather conditions, potentially disrupting:

- 🛰️ Satellite operations
- 🧭 Navigation systems (GPS)
- 📡 Communication infrastructure
- ⚡ Terrestrial power grids (in extreme cases)

Despite continuous monitoring of the Sun by space-based observatories, reliable prediction of the exact onset time of solar flares remains a significant challenge. Many flare models suggest that the coronal magnetic field evolves *gradually* toward an instability threshold, after which rapid energy release occurs.

From a dynamical-systems perspective, such transitions may be preceded by statistical signatures such as enhanced fluctuations, variance growth, or changes in the temporal correlation structure of observable signals. Detecting these signatures in solar monitoring data could therefore provide useful **early indicators** of flare activity.

## 2 · Data and Observations

The analysis is based on publicly available solar monitoring data from the **Geostationary Operational Environmental Satellites (GOES)** program operated by NOAA.

GOES satellites continuously monitor the solar soft X-ray flux in two wavelength channels:

| Channel | Wavelength | Primary Use |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| Short   | 0.5–4 Å   | Flare classification |
| Long    | 1–8 Å     | Primary flare proxy |

For this study we consider time series of the **1–8 Å soft X-ray flux**, which serves as the primary indicator of solar flare activity. Flare event times and classifications are obtained from the NOAA solar flare catalogue, which provides standardized records of:

- Flare start time & peak time
- Duration
- Intensity class: **C**, **M**, and **X**-class events

All datasets are publicly accessible through the [NOAA Space Weather Prediction Center](https://services.swpc.noaa.gov/json/).

## 3 · Statistical Detection Method

Let `F(t)` denote the observed X-ray flux time series. For a given time window of length `T`, the local variability of the signal is quantified using a **windowed variance**:

```
         1   T
σ²_T(t) = ─  Σ  [ F(tᵢ) − F̄_T ]²
         T  i=1
```

where `F̄_T` denotes the mean flux within the window.

Enhanced fluctuations in `σ²_T(t)` may indicate increased dynamical activity in the solar corona preceding flare onset.

To capture combined signatures of pre-flare dynamics, we define a **composite instability indicator**:

```
I(t) = α · σ²_T(t)  +  β · M(t)
```

where `M(t)` represents a surrogate measure of magnetic perturbations or related activity indicators, and `α`, `β` are weighting coefficients.

## 4 · Solar Physics Background

Magnetic activity in the solar atmosphere originates from magnetic fields generated by convective plasma motions inside the Sun. Under appropriate conditions, oppositely directed magnetic field lines can reconnect, converting stored magnetic energy into plasma heating, electromagnetic radiation, and kinetic energy of accelerated particles.

### 4.2 · Energy Distribution

Observational studies of flare statistics suggest that solar flare energies approximately follow **power-law distributions**:

```
P(E) ∼ E⁻ᵅ
```

This is consistent with models of **self-organized criticality** in magnetized plasma systems. In such systems, the gradual accumulation of magnetic stress can lead to avalanche-like reconnection events that release energy across a broad range of spatial and temporal scales.

## 8 · Instability Metrics and Triadic Operator Extension

Solar flare initiation is a strongly nonlinear plasma instability that develops over extended temporal intervals. Precursor signatures may emerge not only in the **amplitude** of observables, but also in their:

- Structural variability
- Informational complexity  
- Cross-channel synchronization

### 8.1 · Variance-Based Instability Baseline

As a baseline diagnostic, we compute the **rolling variance** of the soft X-ray flux `X(t)` over a sliding window of length `L`:

```
           1   L-1
Var_L[X](t) = ─  Σ  [ X(t−i) − X̄_L(t) ]²
           L  i=0
```

with the rolling mean:

```
           1   L-1
X̄_L(t) = ─  Σ  X(t−i)
           L  i=0
```

A composite empirical indicator integrating multiple channels:

```
I(t) = w₁ · Var_L[X](t)  +  w₂ · Var_L[B](t)  +  w₃ · |d/dt EUV(t)|
```

where the weights `w₁, w₂, w₃` are calibrated using historical flare catalogues.

---

### 8.2 · Triadic Instability Operator

Motivated by cross-domain instability analysis in complex dynamical systems, we introduce a **triadic instability functional**:

```
ΔΦ(t) = α|ΔS(t)| + β|ΔI(t)| + γ|ΔC(t)|
```

Where the three components are:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                  TRIADIC OPERATOR COMPONENTS                │
├──────────┬──────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  S(t)    │ Structural variability of coronal magnetic       │
│          │ configuration (magnetometer proxies, magnetic    │
│          │ stress indicators)                               │
├──────────┼──────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  I(t)    │ Informational complexity of the radiative signal │
│          │ (entropy, higher-order variability of X-ray flux)│
├──────────┼──────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  C(t)    │ Cross-channel coherence between GOES X-ray bands │
│          │ and auxiliary channels (EUV ↔ X-ray)             │
└──────────┴──────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### 8.3 · Memory Effects and Non-Markovian Dynamics

Solar active regions exhibit strong **hysteresis**: magnetic energy can accumulate for hours or days before reconnection releases it in a flare event. This implies the underlying dynamical process is intrinsically **non-Markovian**.

The memory component is represented by a slow variable `χ(t)` encoding accumulated magnetic stress:

```
  Time ──────────────────────────────────────────────────►
            Stress builds...              Flare!
  χ(t)  ___/‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾╲___
            hours to days of memory       ↑ rapid release
```

### 8.5 · Falsification Test

A key prediction of the framework: if historical information is artificially removed (e.g., by temporal shuffling), the predictive power of `ΔΦ` should **degrade significantly**. This provides a direct falsification criterion.

> *A purely memoryless description cannot fully encode the accumulated magnetic stress history of an active region.*

## 9 · Operator-Based Non-Equilibrium Interpretation (Spiral-Time Support)

The solar activity signal is treated as an effective non-equilibrium trajectory with memory, embedded into a structured **phase–memory coordinate system**:

```
ψ(t) = t  +  i·φ(t)  +  j·χ(t)
         ↑         ↑          ↑
     chronological  phase-like  slow memory /
        time        coherence   hysteresis
                   coordinate   (magnetic stress)
```

Within this representation, approach to a flare corresponds to:

```
Memory-driven amplification
        +
Cross-channel coherence
        │
        ▼
Enhanced variability + accelerated regime transitions → ☄️ FLARE
```

## 12 · Discussion

The framework proposed in this study is **intentionally conservative** — relying primarily on standard rolling statistical measures and explicit event alignment. The spiral-time operator formalism is used only as a structured interpretive framework, without modifying the underlying forecasting rule.

### Core Contribution

> The central contribution is the **shift from purely amplitude-based monitoring** of solar X-ray flux toward a **trajectory-based analysis** of the temporal evolution of solar activity signals.

### Context in the Literature

| Approach | Method |
|----------|--------|
| Traditional | Static thresholds / rate-of-change on X-ray flux |
| Image-based | Spatial Fourier analysis of SDO/AIA observations |
| ML-based | Magnetogram parameters from SDO/HMI (FLARECAST) |
| **This work** | **Rolling variance + triadic instability functional** |

### Physical Interpretation

The pre-flare solar corona behaves as a **complex dynamical system** approaching a critical transition. In nonlinear systems, increases in variance and fluctuation amplitude are commonly observed prior to instability onset — a phenomenon well-studied in:

- 🌱 Ecological regime shifts
- 🌡️ Climate transitions
- ⚡ Plasma instabilities

The variance growth in X-ray flux time series may reflect increasing dynamical activity as the magnetic system approaches instability thresholds via **kink instability**, **torus instability**, or **tearing-mode reconnection**.

### 12.1 · Interpretation of the Flare-Prediction Evaluation

> **Important caveat:** In the current implementation, flare-like events are derived from the same X-ray channel that also enters the precursor construction. The resulting ROC/AUC values therefore cannot yet be interpreted as a strictly independent measure of operational forecasting performance.

The current experiment provides evidence for **physically meaningful precursor structure** in the GOES X-ray time series. A fully independent assessment will require the pipeline to be evaluated against an external NOAA flare catalogue matched to identical UTC intervals.

### 12.2 · Spiral-Time State Embedding

The triadic state representation

```
ψ(t) = t + i·φ(t) + j·χ(t)
```

offers potential advantages:

| Component | Physical Meaning | Advantage |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------|
| `φ(t)` | Phase coherence | Detects coherence degradation before amplitude rises |
| `χ(t)` | Rate of coherence change | Sensitive to rapid structural changes (increasing twist) |
| Combined | Non-Markovian path | Distinguishes random fluctuations from structured instability |

*Cross-domain applicability:* Related operator-based formulations have been applied to neurodynamic EEG time-series analysis for regime-transition detection in biological systems (Krüger, Medinformatics, accepted/in production), demonstrating the generality of the approach.

---

## 13 · Limitations

> ⚠️ The present analysis should be interpreted as a **proof-of-concept demonstration** rather than a complete statistical validation.

- Only a limited observation window is analyzed here
- Variance growth alone cannot uniquely determine the underlying physical trigger
- A full validation requires large-scale analysis across extended GOES archives
- Comparison with established flare prediction methods is needed
- Statistical indicators presented here should be considered **complementary diagnostics** rather than predictive deterministic models

---

## 14 · Conclusion

We presented a multi-channel instability screening framework for GOES solar observations based on rolling variance diagnostics and explicit flare event overlays.

**Key properties of the framework:**
- ✅ Lightweight and computationally efficient
- ✅ Compatible with operational space-weather monitoring pipelines
- ✅ Flexible: combines X-ray variability with complementary channels
- ✅ Scalable to multi-year GOES archives
- ✅ Extensible to spectral sideband structure, multiscale entropy, non-Markovian memory-kernel diagnostics

**Future work will focus on:**
- Extending analysis to longer GOES archives spanning multiple solar cycles
- Evaluating lead-time distributions, false-alarm rates, and forecasting skill
- Stratifying results by flare class (C, M, X) and solar cycle phase
- Independent validation against fully external flare catalogues
